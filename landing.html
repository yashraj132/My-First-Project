Being a Campus Ambassador is a voluntary role and usually requires a commitment of no more than 2 hours/week on average. As a Campus Ambassador, you decide how to increase awareness of the programme in your university or college, on a time that suits your schedule. Promoting on campus can often involve methods such as putting up posters, circulating flyers, sharing updates on social media groups, running an information evening or stand, speaking to college officials and Heads of Departments, contacting the Studentsâ€™ Union - along with any other creative, innovative strategy you come up with!

As a Campus Ambassador, you are ensuring that as many students as possible hear about this opportunity. This passion and self-motivation will stand out to employers.

Campus Ambassadors have full freedom to coordinate their promotional strategy as they wish.

Campus Ambassador who shows the most innovation and initiative is invited to attend the annuala Global Summit.
